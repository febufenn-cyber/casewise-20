# Evidence and claim-support contract

An evidence item is matter material identified from exact source spans. Its existence does not prove the proposition for which a party cites or relies on it.

Evidence relationships are stored separately from support verification. Supported relationships include supports, contradicts, contextualizes, mentioned by, and relied upon for. Verification states distinguish source verified from support verified, partially supported, unsupported, ambiguous, and unreviewed.

A real quotation may fail to support the proposed conclusion. Reviewers must be able to confirm the source location while rejecting entailment or scope. No evidence relationship becomes attorney-approved merely because it was created by extraction or because a citation opens successfully.
