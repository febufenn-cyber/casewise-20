# Contradiction and missing-information contract

A contradiction candidate links two separately sourced objects that appear incompatible. It is not a confirmed contradiction until a reviewer decides that the conflict is confirmed rather than explained, duplicative, a false positive, or unresolved.

Every candidate preserves object identities, exact source spans for both sides, conflict type, materiality, explanation, warnings, creation method, and review history. Structured date and amount differences may generate proposals, but no automatic proposal becomes a legal conclusion.

A missing-information item means required or referenced material was not located within the documented processed scope. It does not prove nonexistence. Referenced-document items must record what scope was searched. Missing items remain reviewable questions and may be resolved, dismissed, corrected, or left unresolved.
