# Design-Partner and Paid-Pilot Plan

## Objective

Validate the first job with observed workflows, real review behavior, measurable time savings, and a credible payment signal. Compliments and hypothetical interest are not sufficient evidence.

## Target cohort

Recruit 5–10 candidates, aiming for at least:

- two boutique commercial litigators;
- one arbitration lawyer;
- one junior associate or senior paralegal who performs the work;
- one partner or firm owner who controls purchasing or review.

## Qualified design partner

A qualified partner is willing to do at least two of the following:

- reconstruct a recent matter-onboarding workflow;
- show the actual Word, Excel, or note format currently produced;
- provide a public, synthetic, redacted, or explicitly consented matter pack;
- review Casewise output at object and source level;
- record corrections and severity;
- test a second matter or later filing;
- discuss a concrete pilot price and approval path.

## Recruitment message hypothesis

> We are testing a source-linked matter-review workspace for commercial litigation and arbitration teams. It turns pleadings and annexures into a document map, disputed chronology, allegation-response matrix, contradiction list, and missing-information register. The purpose is to reduce first-review and re-reading time without hiding uncertainty or replacing lawyer approval.

Do not lead with “autonomous legal AI” or promise final drafting.

## Interview sequence

### Session 1 — workflow evidence

- reconstruct the last real matter from receipt to partner review;
- inspect actual work products and file organization;
- measure rough time by step;
- identify errors and rework;
- identify the most valuable and most dangerous possible outputs;
- map confidentiality and procurement blockers.

### Session 2 — paper prototype

- present the structured output contract without a polished chatbot;
- ask the user to prioritize or remove sections;
- test whether citations and review states are understandable;
- ask the user to identify what would still require re-reading;
- confirm the minimum useful first workflow.

### Session 3 — matter-pack review

- process or manually simulate one pack;
- compare against the user's existing note;
- record accepted, edited, rejected, and omitted items;
- measure review time;
- identify material failures;
- determine whether a second matter is justified.

### Session 4 — commercial decision

- discuss deployment and data requirements;
- agree scope, page limits, support, and exclusions;
- propose a time-bound paid pilot;
- define success and termination criteria;
- identify the buyer and approval process.

## Evidence to capture

- firm and role profile;
- matter frequency and average bundle size;
- baseline hours by workflow step;
- existing work-product examples;
- most damaging error types;
- upload and confidentiality constraints;
- output priority ranking;
- review time and correction counts;
- repeat-use behavior;
- pricing and procurement signal;
- direct quotations with permission and no confidential content.

## Design-partner selection

Use `research/design-partner-scorecard.csv`. Prioritize pain, frequency, review discipline, access to permissible packs, repeated engagement, and payment signal. A friendly but non-operational lawyer is not automatically a good partner.

## Paid pilot hypothesis

Initial structure:

- 30–45 days;
- one firm or team;
- defined supported matter type;
- 1–3 matter packs within agreed page limits;
- founder-assisted onboarding;
- explicit confidentiality and data terms;
- no autonomous filing or final pleading;
- weekly review session;
- fixed pilot fee or per-matter fee;
- written success and stop conditions.

The pilot must distinguish founder service time from software value.

## Pilot success criteria

- at least one usable matter map produced;
- source links and coverage warnings are trusted;
- measurable net time savings after review;
- no material cross-matter or citation-integrity failures;
- reviewer can correct objects without losing lineage;
- user requests or performs a second use;
- buyer indicates a viable renewal or conversion path.

## Pilot stop conditions

- confidentiality terms cannot be met;
- material source errors cannot be reviewed efficiently;
- partner review time exceeds baseline preparation savings;
- output requires uncontrolled custom legal analysis;
- the customer demands prohibited autonomy;
- processing economics are incompatible with price;
- no repeated use after the first novelty session.

## Ethical data handling

Interview notes must not contain unnecessary confidential facts. Matter packs require documented provenance and permission. Redaction must be performed by or approved by the data owner; Casewise must not assume an automated redaction is sufficient for repository inclusion.
