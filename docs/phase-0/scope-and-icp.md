# Initial Scope and Ideal Customer Profile

## Customer hypothesis

### Firm profile

Boutique Indian commercial-litigation and arbitration practices with approximately 2–15 lawyers.

### Primary user

A junior associate or senior paralegal responsible for understanding a newly received matter bundle and preparing the first internal note.

### Economic buyer

A partner, practice head, or firm owner responsible for review quality, turnaround time, staffing, and legal-technology spend.

### Trigger event

A new matter, transferred matter, large filing, rejoinder, amended pleading, or new evidence bundle arrives and a team member must reconstruct the matter before substantive drafting or strategy work begins.

## Job to be done

When a case bundle arrives, help the legal team produce a reliable first matter map faster, with less re-reading and a clear trail from every material statement to the source.

The job is not described as “use AI.” The replacement work product is a structured, reviewable internal matter note.

## Supported initial documents

- plaints, petitions, and statements of claim;
- written statements, replies, and statements of defence;
- rejoinders and amended pleadings;
- affidavits;
- contracts and amendments;
- invoices, notices, letters, and correspondence;
- annexures and exhibits;
- court or tribunal orders;
- searchable English PDFs;
- scanned English PDFs containing predominantly printed text.

## Supported bundle profile

- one active matter per workspace;
- English-language commercial disputes;
- approximately 1–2,000 pages during pilots, with larger bundles requiring explicit review;
- PDFs that may contain multiple logical documents;
- mixed native-text and scanned pages;
- documents requiring source-page verification.

## Explicitly unsupported in the first release

- direct-to-consumer legal advice;
- autonomous court or tribunal filing;
- filing-ready final pleadings;
- outcome or judge prediction;
- automatic settlement valuation;
- criminal guilt or civil-liability determinations;
- live external legal research;
- regional-language primary workflows;
- audio or video evidence analysis;
- handwriting as the principal evidence source;
- native email archives and broad e-discovery collections;
- scientific or forensic expert-evidence interpretation;
- password-protected or active-content files;
- cross-matter knowledge reuse.

Unsupported content must be rejected or isolated with an explanation. It must not be processed partially without visible coverage warnings.

## First value proposition

> Upload the core pleadings and annexures. Casewise builds a source-linked document register, party map, chronology, allegation-response matrix, contradiction register, and list of missing information for lawyer review.

## Differentiation hypothesis

Casewise should not compete by being another blank legal chatbot. The defensible product shape is:

- exact evidence provenance;
- explicit allegation-versus-fact distinctions;
- structured matter memory;
- exception-focused human review;
- later filing-to-filing delta analysis;
- durable correction and approval history.

## Non-target customers for initial validation

- consumers seeking legal answers;
- large enterprise legal departments requiring procurement and integrations before any trial;
- firms requiring on-premises deployment as a precondition for discovery;
- practices dominated by non-English or handwritten materials;
- teams seeking only general case-law search;
- users unwilling to review AI-assisted output.

## Open hypotheses to validate

1. Chronology and allegation-response mapping create enough value before drafting.
2. Junior time saved does not create equal or greater partner-review time.
3. Firms will provide public, synthetic, redacted, or consented matter packs.
4. Cloud processing can meet pilot confidentiality requirements.
5. The buyer prefers subscription-plus-pages or per-matter pricing over pure per-seat pricing.
6. Commercial litigation and arbitration are sufficiently repeatable for a shared ontology.
