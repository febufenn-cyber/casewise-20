# Unit Economics and Pricing Hypotheses

## Purpose

Casewise must save more professional time than it consumes and process matters at a cost compatible with a credible paid product. Phase 0 defines the accounting model; real values must be populated during prototype and pilot runs.

## Matter-size bands

| Band | Pages | Pilot handling |
|---|---:|---|
| Small | 1–100 | normal |
| Medium | 101–500 | normal |
| Large | 501–2,000 | cost and queue warning |
| Very large | 2,001+ | explicit approval or later tier |

## Cost ledger per matter

Track separately:

- original and derived storage;
- file validation and malware scanning;
- page rendering and normalization;
- native text extraction;
- OCR pages and alternate OCR passes;
- document segmentation;
- embeddings or indexes;
- extraction model input/output;
- verifier and challenger passes;
- regeneration after correction;
- export generation;
- provider/network overhead;
- failed-job retries;
- customer support and onboarding time;
- human quality-review time during pilots.

## Required measurements

For every processing run record:

- page count by native text versus OCR;
- model calls and tokens by operation;
- duration and retries;
- total variable infrastructure cost;
- manual intervention time;
- reviewer time before and after Casewise;
- number and severity of corrections;
- cost per accepted work product.

## Value model

The economic value hypothesis is based on:

- reduced junior/paralegal preparation time;
- reduced repeated reading when a new filing arrives;
- reduced partner rework caused by unsupported or poorly sourced notes;
- faster matter handoff;
- faster source verification during review;
- persistent matter memory.

Do not count “hours generated” as value. Measure net time saved after review and correction.

## Pricing hypotheses

### A. Per matter

Easy to connect to a discrete case-bundle review. Useful for early paid pilots and firms with irregular usage.

### B. Subscription plus included pages

Likely long-term candidate:

- workspace or user fee;
- monthly included processing allowance;
- additional page or matter charges;
- higher tier for restricted matters, retention controls, and advanced review.

### C. Per seat

Simple, but potentially misaligned when document volume varies sharply. The original $59–129 per-seat concept remains an unvalidated hypothesis.

### D. Concierge service

A temporary pilot model in which onboarding and output review are founder-assisted. It may accelerate learning but must track service labour separately so a service-heavy model is not mistaken for software margin.

## Preliminary economic gates

Proceed toward self-serve pricing only when:

- variable processing cost is measured on small, medium, and large packs;
- net professional time savings remain positive after review;
- a realistic price supports a target gross margin after ordinary support;
- retries and OCR-heavy matters do not create uncontrolled tail costs;
- at least one buyer accepts a paid-pilot structure.

## Pivot signals

- customers value a per-matter managed service more than software;
- large bundles require specialist review that cannot be reduced;
- private deployment is mandatory and economically dominant;
- page volume rather than seats predicts value and cost;
- only the filing-delta feature produces recurring value.

## Open decisions

- target gross margin;
- included page allowance;
- treatment of failed or unsupported pages;
- retention/storage surcharge;
- pricing for later-filing delta runs;
- whether reviewers or all users require paid seats;
- pilot discount and conversion terms.
