# Design-Partner Interview Guide

## Interview objective

Reconstruct actual behavior and collect decision-grade evidence. Do not pitch for most of the session. Avoid asking whether the participant “likes AI” or would hypothetically use a polished future product.

## Before the call

Record:

- participant role and seniority;
- firm size and practice area;
- whether the participant performs, reviews, or buys the workflow;
- permission to take notes;
- prohibition on sharing unnecessary confidential facts;
- whether they can show a sanitized work product or file structure.

## Opening

> I am studying how litigation and arbitration teams understand a new matter bundle and prepare the first internal note. I want to learn the current workflow, including what is slow, risky, and repeatedly corrected. Please avoid sharing privileged or identifying details unless you have authority and a safe way to do so.

## Section 1 — Last real matter

- Tell me about the last matter bundle you had to understand from scratch.
- What triggered the work?
- What arrived first, and through which channels?
- Roughly how many files and pages were involved?
- Were they searchable PDFs, scans, mixed bundles, or something else?
- Who performed the first review?
- What did that person need to deliver?
- How long did each step take?
- What changed when the partner reviewed it?

Follow-up prompts:

- What did you copy into Word or Excel?
- How did you identify document boundaries in a combined PDF?
- How did you track annexures or missing documents?
- How did you distinguish allegations from admitted facts?
- How did you revisit the work when a later filing arrived?

## Section 2 — Show the work

Ask for sanitized examples where permitted:

- file naming and folder structure;
- chronology template;
- internal matter note;
- allegation/response table;
- partner comments;
- source-reference convention;
- document or evidence index.

Questions:

- Which parts are mandatory?
- Which parts are copied from older templates?
- Which parts are frequently wrong?
- Which parts does the partner verify first?
- Where is source provenance lost?

## Section 3 — Failure and trust

- What is the most damaging mistake in this workflow?
- Is a false positive or missed fact worse? Give an example.
- What would make you stop using a tool after one matter?
- Which items would you always verify manually?
- How do you currently verify a junior's note?
- What does “complete” mean for this workflow?
- How do you handle unreadable pages or missing annexures?
- Would exact page highlights materially reduce review time?
- What must the system say when it is uncertain?

## Section 4 — Confidentiality and deployment

- What kinds of matter documents can be used in a pilot?
- Can you use public, synthetic, professionally redacted, or consented materials?
- Who decides whether cloud processing is permitted?
- What provider, retention, region, deletion, or support-access questions would block a pilot?
- Are ethical walls or restricted matters common?
- Does a firm administrator automatically have access to every matter today?
- What evidence would you need before using the product with confidential content?

Do not promise that current architecture meets requirements. Capture them.

## Section 5 — Test the output contract

Present the proposed outputs individually:

1. document register;
2. party/entity map;
3. disputed chronology;
4. allegation-response matrix;
5. contradiction register;
6. missing-information register;
7. internal overview;
8. later-filing change report.

For each ask:

- Is this created today?
- Who creates and reviews it?
- How much time does it consume?
- What must be exact?
- What would make it unusable?
- Would you pay for this without final drafting?
- Rank it from essential to unnecessary.

## Section 6 — Commercial evidence

- Who would approve a pilot?
- Is value measured per matter, page, user, or month?
- Does the workflow consume billable, non-billable, or administrative time?
- What would a successful 30–45 day pilot prove?
- What could you safely provide for testing?
- Would you test a second matter if the first meets the criteria?
- What budget path exists?
- Would you prefer a fixed pilot fee, per-matter fee, or subscription with page allowance?

Ask for a concrete next action, not enthusiasm.

## Evidence rating after the call

Mark each as Yes, Partial, or No:

- actual recent workflow reconstructed;
- actual work product observed;
- baseline time estimated;
- dangerous errors identified;
- permissible matter-pack path identified;
- repeated-use case identified;
- buyer and procurement path identified;
- participant agreed to another session;
- participant accepted a paid-pilot discussion.

## Red flags

- participant speaks only in generalities;
- no responsibility for performing, reviewing, or buying the workflow;
- enthusiasm without a file, artifact, second meeting, or payment path;
- demands autonomous filing or guaranteed completeness;
- refuses all permissible test-data options;
- wants a general chatbot rather than the defined job;
- cannot identify a recurring workflow.
